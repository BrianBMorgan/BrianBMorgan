I started on the design side, shipping visual systems for teams that had strong ideas and thin design support. Filling that gap became the job. Over the years it grew into a practice that runs on three lanes: building products, producing the events that launch them, and drawing the identities that hold everything together.

The through-line is endurance and operational clarity under ambiguity. I'd rather ship an experience that has long-lived impact than ten that fade into history. That bias shows up in how I work: strategy before execution, documentation as part of the deliverable, and a strong preference for taste that outlives trend cycles.

## How I work

- **Small teams, direct lines.** I work best embedded with the people who own the problem: no layers, no telephone.
- **Working systems over theater.** Purpose-driven beats spectacle. A prototype or plan that lands and sticks.
- **The last five percent.** Easing curves, small touches, audits: the details nobody budgets for are the ones people remember.

## Now

I take on a handful of projects each quarter from my studio in Portland. Between engagements I write, speak, and keep a workshop habit: the [thought leadership](/thought-leadership) section is where that thinking lands.
