Rivet builds settlement infrastructure — serious software sold to serious buyers. The identity had to signal precision without borrowing the visual clichés of fintech: no gradients pretending to be innovation, no abstract network diagrams.

## The work

The mark is a single riveted joint, drawn on the same 4px grid the product uses. From there the system unrolls: a token architecture covering color, type, and spacing; a React component library; and the documentation that makes both usable by people who never met me.

<Figure src="/images/work/rivet-tokens.svg" alt="Rivet token architecture from brand decisions down to component styles" caption="ONE DECISION, MANY SURFACES · TOKENS CARRY THE BRAND INTO THE PRODUCT" />

Brand and product shipped from the same tokens on day one — the marketing site and the app disagree about nothing.

## What endured

- The engagement handed off in eight weeks; the team has shipped 3 product surfaces since without a design hire.
- The documentation is the contract: every component page states what the pattern is for and, more importantly, what it is not for.
