Atlas runs small expeditions and wanted the companion guide to feel like equipment — something you pack, annotate, and keep. Not a brochure.

## The work

120 pages, set in a serif drawn for the project and printed on stock chosen to take pencil. The grid holds photography, route maps, and long-form writing in one system, with mono-set marginalia carrying coordinates and dates.

Production was half the design: paper tests, ink-density proofs, and a binding that lies flat in the field.

## What endured

- The guide is on its third printing, unchanged.
- The typeface later became the seed of Atlas's wider brand voice — the print piece led the identity rather than following it.
